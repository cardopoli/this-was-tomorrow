I'm visiting in May 2026. Contact is Paul Brannigan in Hebden Bridge.

## The place

Calder Valley Community Land Trust was established in 2014 in the Upper Calder Valley - Todmorden, Hebden Bridge, Mytholmroyd - a post-industrial landscape of steep hills and mill towns in West Yorkshire. The CLT is a community benefit society with charitable status, governed by over 300 members who each hold one non-transferable £1 share.

One of its most significant projects is on Hebden Bridge High Street - the site of housing cleared during 1960s slum clearances, over 70 dwellings demolished to leave a small tarmacked lane. Several people living in Hebden Bridge today remember those homes. Planning permission for 20 affordable rented homes on this site was granted in 2023. The street is coming back.

## How it works

Land transferred by Calderdale Council at nil cost removes it from the affordability equation. Projects are developed in partnership with Connect Housing and built to Passivhaus standard - low energy, high insulation, air source heat pumps, solar panels. The CLT retains ownership; Connect manages tenancies. Members elect ten volunteer trustees who govern the organisation. Homes are let via the council's Keychoice system to local people in housing need.

## Why I'm going

The valley has a history of bottom-up organising - Hebden Bridge Town Hall was the first community asset transfer in the country. I want to understand what it looks and feels like to bring back a street that was erased, and whether the people building these homes have any connection to the people whose homes were demolished.
