body_intro: |-
  Trevone Quarry was worked for granite from the early 1800s until the mid-1980s. In 2009, Rob Higgs acquired the 13-acre post-industrial site outside Penryn. Over the following years, eleven acres were planted with broadleaf mixed woodland. Slowly, a community formed.

  The site now holds several things at once: a residential community living in self-built structures and caravans; a cluster of creative businesses occupying the former quarry buildings; a rewilding and species reintroduction project; and a working agro-forest farm. The hope is that these four things benefit each other the way an ecosystem does.
