## The place

Treneere is a council estate on the outskirts of Penzance, built in the 1930s as part of the same wave of slum clearance that produced Gwavas in nearby Newlyn. Most housing is managed by Penwith Housing Association. In 2017 an Index of Multiple Deprivation report described Treneere as the most deprived neighbourhood in Cornwall. Built as a solution to one crisis, designated a problem area in another, managed from outside ever since.

## How it works

Managed social housing. Whole Again Communities - a local organisation working on community resilience and sustainable living - operates in and around the estate. They are the reason this site is in the project.

## Why I'm going

Because the contrast between this estate and the community-led projects elsewhere is not incidental to the project - it is part of the argument. What does housing look like when the community had no say in it? What accumulates over 90 years of that? And what are Whole Again Communities doing in this specific place that makes it worth photographing now?
