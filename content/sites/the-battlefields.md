The Battlefields doesn't have a website. It doesn't appear in the community housing literature or the CLT network directories. What it has is Tim Dwelly - Independent councillor for Penzance East, Portfolio Holder for Economy at Cornwall Council - who mentioned it in the course of a conversation about what community-led activity in Penzance actually looks like on the ground, and said he could show me himself. That offer carries its own meaning.

There's a specific kind of knowledge that sits with the people who've been watching a town for decades: the places that appear in no report but that matter enormously to the people in them. Tim Dwelly has been involved in Penzance's housing and regeneration landscape long enough to know those places. The Battlefields is one of them.

I don't know yet what I'll find. That's the point of going. What I do know is that arriving somewhere with a local guide who has real institutional knowledge - and real stake in the town - changes what you see and what people are willing to say. It's a different entry point to a visit than a cold outreach email and a project information sheet.

## The Tim Dwelly Connection

Dwelly is one of the more interesting figures in Cornwall's regeneration story - long enough in post (elected Labour in 2013, now Independent) to have watched multiple regeneration strategies succeed and fail, embedded enough in the town to be trusted by communities rather than just by the council, and sceptical enough of orthodoxy to be genuinely useful to think with. He was directly involved in the Treneere work, is across the Whole Again Communities / WAC activity in Penzance, and introduced me to both Trevone Quarry and The Battlefields.

The framing he gave for The Battlefields is that it's something worth seeing. I'm taking that at face value and going.
