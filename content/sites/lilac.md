## The place

LILAC - Low Impact Living Affordable Community - began in 2006 when five Leeds residents decided to build their own homes and live differently. They established a Mutual Home Ownership Society, broke ground in 2012, and moved in during May 2013. The site is a former school in Bramley, west Leeds: twenty self-contained homes, a mix of flats and houses, built from ModCell - prefabricated timber frame panels filled with straw bales and finished in lime render. Super-insulated, south-facing, car-free.

## How it works

The Mutual Home Ownership model means members pay a collective mortgage rather than individual ones, contributing 35% of net household income. Equity is built and taken away based on a national income formula rather than property values - keeping homes affordable for future generations regardless of what the market does. The common house at the centre includes shared kitchen, laundry, meeting space, guest rooms, and garden. Residents eat together twice a week. The 25-year mortgage is with Triodos Bank.

## Why I'm going

LILAC is the most financially innovative model in the project - the Mutual Home Ownership structure is designed to permanently sever the link between housing and the property market. I want to understand what that actually feels like to live inside. Whether the permanence of the affordability model changes how people relate to the place. Whether eating together twice a week is the point or just a symptom of it.
