## The place

Newquay Community Orchard began as a response to a specific patch of neglected land in the middle of the town. Six months of community consultation, a crowdfund that raised double its £30,000 target, 20,000 volunteer hours, and a land gift from the Duchy of Cornwall later - there is now a seven-acre site with 120 apple trees, a Victorian-style kitchen garden, wildflower meadow, two native woodland areas, a forest garden, a community building, a café, outdoor classrooms, workshops and a makerspace. Open to everyone, free, any time.

## How it works

Run by community interest company Urban Biodiversity. Revenue from the café, events and contracts like Food for Change (teaching unemployed people about growing, cooking and trading food) and the Practical Outdoor Internship (sustainable landscaping training). Power to Change awarded £252,000 to construct the community building. The site supports employment training, mental health therapy, environmental education, and a resident kestrel.

## Why I'm going

Newquay is surf tourism and stag weekends. The orchard is something else entirely - a collective act of making something slow and seasonal in the middle of a town built around consumption and the sea. I want to understand who comes here and why, and what it means to tend an apple tree in a place like that.
